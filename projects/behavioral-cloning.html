Behavioral Cloning in Autonomous Vehicles

Project Overview

In this project, I developed a self-driving car using behavioral cloning, where a neural network learns to drive by mimicking human behavior. Using the Self-Driving Car Simulator in Unity, I collected and augmented training data to improve model performance.

I implemented a Modified LeNet Model in Keras, training it to classify and predict steering actions based on visual input. The final model was successfully tested on a new track, showcasing its ability to navigate autonomously.
