Safety Metrics for Human Aerial Collaboration in presence of Aerodynamic Disturbances

❮ ❯

Project Overview

When aerial robots work closely with humans, wind disturbances can significantly impact the drone's stability and trajectory, creating potential safety hazards. This research simulates a comprehensive safety metric that guides an aerial robot's motion during such interactions, ensuring human safety even in windy conditions.

The metric works by estimating the additional force and deviation caused by wind disturbances, allowing the robot to maintain safe distances from human collaborators. By incorporating this safety metric into a Non-Linear Model Predictive Controller (NMPC), the system generates control inputs that satisfy safety constraints throughout the drone's trajectory.
