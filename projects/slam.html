Project Overview

During my Robotics Internship at Aziobot B.V., I worked on developing self-exploration and mapping capabilities for an autonomous floor-scrubbing robot (SB2). The goal was to enable the robot to navigate and map unknown environments autonomously, eliminating the need for manual intervention. Using SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping) in ROS, I implemented frontier-based exploration with BFS (Breadth-First Search), allowing the robot to detect unexplored areas and plan its path accordingly.

I designed and simulated the robot model in Gazebo, integrating it with the ROS Navigation Stack (move_base) to optimize path planning. Additionally, I worked with LiDAR sensors to enhance perception, ensuring accurate obstacle detection and efficient trajectory. This experience strengthened my expertise in robotics, autonomous navigation, and real-time mapping, contributing to advancements in industrial automation and smart robotics.
